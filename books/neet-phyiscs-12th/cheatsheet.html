The 30-second story Charges fill space with a field. A field pushes charges into a current (whose electrons barely crawl, while the signal flies). A moving charge feels a sideways magnetic force and circles. Change a magnetic flux and you induce a voltage (Faraday), always opposing the change (Lenz). Drive an LCR circuit and it resonates at one frequency. And a field forever remaking itself, cut loose, is light.

Traps that eat marks

  • Current vs. electrons: conventional current points opposite to electron drift.
  • Magnetic force does no work: it only turns v, never changes speed or kinetic energy.
  • Cyclotron frequency: does not depend on v or r — a favourite trick question.
  • Induction needs change: a magnet sitting motionless in a coil induces nothing.
  • Lenz’s minus sign: it’s energy conservation, not algebra decoration.
  • Resonance: at f₀ the reactances cancel, impedance = R (pure), current is maximum.
  • EM waves are transverse: E ⟂ B ⟂ direction of travel, and E, B peak together.

How to use this book before an exam

Don’t re-read — re-play. Open each chapter’s live figure and predict what it will do before you move the slider. Right prediction = you understand it. Wrong = that’s exactly the concept to revise. Ten minutes of that beats an hour of passive reading.

Why: exams test whether you can predict a system’s behaviour, not whether you can recognise a paragraph. The widgets train prediction directly.
