Reading key

Box: strings low E (left) → high e (right); top bar = nut.
Dot number = finger: 1 index · 2 middle · 3 ring · 4 pinky.
O = open · × = don't play · R = root.

Reading key — worked shapes

Minor pentatonic — 5 boxes (A minor: A C D E G)

Frets per string, low E 6 → high e 1. Movable — shift so a 6th-string root = your key.

Box 1 (5): 5-8 · 5-7 · 5-7 · 5-7 · 5-8 · 5-8
Box 2 (7): 8-10 · 7-10 · 7-10 · 7-9 · 8-10 · 8-10
Box 3 (10): 10-12 · 10-12 · 10-12 · 9-12 · 10-13 · 10-12
Box 4 (12): 12-15 · 12-15 · 12-14 · 12-14 · 13-15 · 12-15
Box 5 (15): 15-17 · 15-17 · 14-17 · 14-17 · 15-17 · 15-17

Roots (A): Box1 → 6/5, 4/7, 1/5. The five boxes loop: after Box 5 comes Box 1 an octave up.

Major pentatonic

Formula 1 2 3 5 6. Same five shapes as minor pentatonic.
To make a minor box major (same letter): shift it down 3 frets.
A minor pent = C major pent (identical notes). Rest on the major root for the sunny sound.

Blues scale

Formula 1 ♭3 4 ♭5 5 ♭7 = minor pentatonic + the ♭5 blue note.
A blues = A C D E♭ E G. In Box 1 add E♭ at 5th-str/6 and 3rd-str/8.
The ♭5 is a passing note — slide 4→♭5→5, never land on it.

30-second summary

Five chord shapes (C A G E D) tile the neck. Barre them to move any chord anywhere.
Five pentatonic boxes tile the neck the same way. Box 1 first; connect the rest.
Major pentatonic = the same boxes, different root. Add one ♭5 and it's the blues.
Practise everything with the metronome, slow, until it's even — then speed up.
