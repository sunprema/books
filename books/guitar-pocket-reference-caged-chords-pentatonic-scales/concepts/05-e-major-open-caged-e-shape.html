Full, ringing, and the single most important shape — it becomes the 6th-string barre chord.

Shape

  • 0 2 2 1 0 0 — index on G/1, middle on A/2, ring on D/2.
  • Low E, B and high e all ring open.
  • Root E on the 6th (low E) string, open.

When & why

  • Bedrock of blues, rock and the key of E.
  • E–A–B is the 12-bar blues in E.
  • Its 6th-string root is why the E-form barre names any chord off the low E.

Moves & transitions

  • To A: the E→A→B shuffle — keep the hand relaxed.
  • To Em: just lift the index — one finger away.
  • Barre it at fret 1 → F (see the E-form page).
Practice tipLet the index sit close behind the 1st fret on the G string so it never leans onto the open B.

“Master E cleanly. Barre chords are just this shape with your index playing the nut.”
