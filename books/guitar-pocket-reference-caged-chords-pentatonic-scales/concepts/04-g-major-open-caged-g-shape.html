Big and six-string wide. All strings ring; roots top and bottom.

Shape

  • 3 2 0 0 0 3 — middle on low E/3, index on A/2, ring on high e/3.
  • The D, G and B strings ring open in the middle.
  • Roots G on the 6th and 1st strings.

When & why

  • The home chord of the key of G — folk, country, pop.
  • G–C–D and G–Em–C–D are everywhere.
  • A 4-finger version 3 2 0 0 3 3 gives a fuller, chunkier top.

Moves & transitions

  • To C: use fingers 2–3–4 for G so 1–2–3 drop straight onto C.
  • To D: G→D is the workhorse country change.
  • To Em: slide middle+ring back one fret.
Practice tipKeep the wrist forward and knuckles arched so the three open middle strings all ring.

“The 3–2–0–0–3–3 grip changes to C faster than the folk shape. Learn both.”
