Three fingers crammed into one fret. Compact, punchy, and the model for the 5th-string barre.

Shape

  • x 0 2 2 2 0 — fingers 1–2–3 across D, G, B at the 2nd fret.
  • A rings open below; high e rings open above; low E is muted.
  • Root A on the 5th (A) string, open.

When & why

  • The other everyday major; core of the keys A, D and E.
  • Rock and blues live on the A–D–E shapes.
  • Its 5th-string root becomes the A-form barre chord.

Moves & transitions

  • To D: lift off and re-form the compact top-string triangle.
  • To E: shift the same 3-finger block down one string set.
  • Try fingers 2–3–4 instead — it frees the index for quick barre-ups.
Practice tipSqueeze three fingers into one fret by stacking them on their tips so the open high e still rings.

“If the high e chokes, your fingers are lying flat. Arch them — tips only.”
