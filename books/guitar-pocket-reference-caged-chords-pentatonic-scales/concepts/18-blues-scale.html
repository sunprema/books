Minor pentatonic plus one note: the ♭5 blue note. That extra note is the whole sound of the blues.

Shape

  • Blues scale = 1 ♭3 4 ♭5 5 ♭7. A blues = A C D E♭ E G.
  • It's Box 1 + the ♭5: add E♭ on the 5th str 6th fret and 3rd str 8th fret.
  • The blue notes (♭) are marked in blue on the diagram.

When & why

  • Blues, rock, and any solo that wants grit.
  • The ♭5 is tension — it makes the resolution feel earned.
  • Learn it as 'minor pentatonic with two extra dots'.

Moves & transitions

  • Slide or hammer through the ♭5 into the 4 or 5 — don't sit on it.
  • Works over dominant blues in A (A7–D7–E7).
  • The other boxes take the ♭5 too, but these two spots are the usable ones.
Practice tipThe ♭5 is a passing note, not a landing note. Practise gliding 4→♭5→5 in time so it never sounds like a wrong note.

“One note turns the pentatonic into the blues. Use it like hot sauce — a touch, in passing, never a whole mouthful.”
