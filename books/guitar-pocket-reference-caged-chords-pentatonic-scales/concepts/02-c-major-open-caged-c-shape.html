Bright, open, everywhere. The C shape is your first anchor and the seed of the movable C-form.

Shape

  • x 3 2 0 1 0 — ring on A/3, middle on D/2, index on B/1.
  • Low E is muted; strings D and high e ring open.
  • Root C sits on the 5th (A) string, 3rd fret.

When & why

  • The go-to open major in the keys of C and G.
  • Half of every I–IV–V in C: C–F–G.
  • Its root-on-5th layout is why the movable C-form keeps that root.

Moves & transitions

  • To Am: keep index and middle planted, lift the ring — nearly the same hand.
  • To G: pivot the ring finger down to the low E, 3rd fret.
  • To F: the classic C→F change — practise it slow.
Practice tipAim the strum to start on the A string, not the low E — that muted string sounds muddy.

“Get the open G and high e ringing clean before you rush the change. One clean chord beats three buzzy ones.”

Fingering: Applied Guitar Theory; D'Addario CAGED.
