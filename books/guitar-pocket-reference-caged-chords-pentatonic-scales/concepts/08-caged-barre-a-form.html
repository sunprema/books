Movable A-form

finger 1–4 O = open · × = don't play root: 5th string 
A-form = B major
e|--2--|
B|--4--|
G|--4--|
D|--4--|
A|--2--|
E|--x--|

One of the two everyday barre chords. Root on the 5th string; index barres, ring barres the middle three.

Shape

  • Shown as B major at fret 2: x 2 4 4 4 2.
  • Index barres strings 5–1; ring flattens across D, G, B two frets up.
  • Root on the 5th string (2nd fret = B).

When & why

  • The 5th-string-root barre — any major named off the A string.
  • B, C#, F, Bb... all the ones with no easy open shape.
  • Pairs with the E-form to cover the whole neck.

Moves & transitions

  • Slide up 2 frets from A-form-Bb... it just moves — same shape, new name.
  • Let the ring's tip mute the high e, or curl to let it ring.
  • Drop the ring barre for a clean power-chord core (x 2 4 4 x x).
Practice tipIf the notes under the ring-finger barre buzz, roll the ring slightly toward the nut and press from the thumb.

“E-form and A-form are the two barres that actually matter. Own these before the other three.”
