root (R) scale note

Two kinds of picture in this book. Learn to read both in 30 seconds and every page is playable.

The chord box

  • Vertical lines = strings. Low E on the left, high e on the right.
  • Horizontal lines = frets. The thick bar on top is the nut.
  • Dot = a finger. The number is which: 1 index, 2 middle, 3 ring, 4 pinky.
  • Above the nut: O = play open, × = mute / don't play.
  • A red R dot marks the root — the note that names it.

The ASCII tab

  • Six lines — high e on top, low E on the bottom (standard tab).
  • A number = the fret to press on that string. 0 = open, x = muted.
  • A chord is one vertical stack; a scale reads left → right, low string to high.
Fret vs. fingerThe box shows frets (where to press); the number inside a dot is the finger. Don't mix them up.
Practice tipFret with the tips of your fingers, knuckles arched, just behind the fret wire. Strum, then fix the one string that buzzes.

“A diagram you can't read is decoration. Spend the half-minute — then everything here plays.”

Reading conventions: Applied Guitar Theory; Guitar Lesson World.
