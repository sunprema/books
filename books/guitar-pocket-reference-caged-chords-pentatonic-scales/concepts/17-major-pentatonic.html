Same five shapes as minor pentatonic — just a different root. Happy, open, country-and-major-key sound.

Shape

  • Major pentatonic = 1 2 3 5 6. G major pent = G A B D E.
  • Box 1 shown at frets 2–5: 3–5 / 2–5 / 2–5 / 2–4 / 3–5 / 3–5.
  • Roots (R): 6th str 3, 4th str 5, 1st str 3.

When & why

  • Country, pop, major-key melodies and bright solos.
  • The shapes are identical to the minor boxes — only the root moves.
  • A minor pentatonic and C major pentatonic are the same notes.

Moves & transitions

  • The trick: to turn a minor box major on the same letter, shift it down 3 frets.
  • Or keep the shape and just treat the major note as home.
  • This G-shape is the same fingering as E minor pentatonic Box 1.
Practice tipPlay the exact Box-1 shape you know, but rest on the major root (the red R). Hear how the mood flips from bluesy to sunny.

“You already know major pentatonic — it's the minor boxes wearing a different hat. Move the root, change the feeling.”
