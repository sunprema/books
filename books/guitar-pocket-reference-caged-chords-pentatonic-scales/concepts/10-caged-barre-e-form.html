The workhorse. Open E barred at the nut = F. Root on the 6th string; slide it anywhere.

Shape

  • Shown as F major at fret 1: 1 3 3 2 1 1.
  • Index barres all six strings; the E shape sits above it.
  • Root on the 6th string (1st fret = F).

When & why

  • Any major named off the low E string — F, G, A, Bb...
  • The first barre chord everyone learns (and fights).
  • Its minor cousin (lift the middle) gives every minor barre too.

Moves & transitions

  • Slide up 2 frets from F → G, another 2 → A. Same shape.
  • Roll the index onto its bony outer edge for a cleaner barre.
  • Squeeze from the thumb behind the neck, not by crushing the hand.
Practice tipBarre only as hard as you must. Strum, find the dead string, and adjust the index roll until all six ring.

“This is E major with your index doing the nut's job. If you can play E, you're one strong index away from F.”
