The one everybody plays. Root on the low E string, so it moves to any key by lining that root up.

Shape

  • A minor pentatonic = A C D E G (1 ♭3 4 5 ♭7).
  • Box 1 sits at the 5th fret: 5–8 / 5–7 / 5–7 / 5–7 / 5–8 / 5–8.
  • Roots (R): 6th str 5, 4th str 7, 1st str 5.

When & why

  • Solos, riffs and licks in A minor / A blues start here.
  • Movable: line the 6th-string root up with your key (fret 3 = G minor, etc.).
  • The reference box the other four hang off.

Moves & transitions

  • Connects up to Box 2 and down to Box 5.
  • Bend the G-string note (7th fret) up a tone — the signature rock bend.
  • Land phrases on a red R for a resolved, 'home' sound.
Practice tipPlay it with the metronome: two notes per click, up and back down, slow. Speed up only when it's even.

“Ninety percent of rock and blues leads live in this box. Learn to make it sing before you collect the others.”
