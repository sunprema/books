A compact triangle on the top four strings. Root on the 4th string, bright and clear.

Shape

  • x x 0 2 3 2 — index on G/2, ring on B/3, middle on high e/2.
  • D rings open; low E and A are not played.
  • Root D on the 4th (D) string, open.

When & why

  • Home chord of the key of D; essential in G and A too.
  • G–D–C and A–D–E lean on it constantly.
  • Its 4th-string root becomes the movable D-form triad.

Moves & transitions

  • To A: A→D is a foundational strum change.
  • To G: land index+middle first, the anchors.
  • Add pinky at high e/3 for the Dsus4 → D pull-off tease.
Practice tipStart the strum on the open D string — catching the A or low E muddies it.

“Keep the ring finger off the high e. If the top string thuds, your ring is flattening onto it.”
