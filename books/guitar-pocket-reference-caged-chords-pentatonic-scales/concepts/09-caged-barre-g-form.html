The hardest CAGED shape to barre. 6th-string root, huge stretch — mostly used as a partial or connector.

Shape

  • Shown as A major: open G moved up 2 frets, 5 4 2 2 2 5.
  • Reach from the low root to the top note is enormous — often a triad only.
  • Root on the 6th string (5th fret = A).

When & why

  • Fills the gap between the E-form and D-form positions.
  • Its top-3 corner is a bright major triad worth stealing.
  • Mainly a fretboard-mapping and arpeggio shape.

Moves & transitions

  • Play the useful bits: the top-string triad, or bass root + top 3.
  • The G and D forms are the two prone to compressing — keep the empty fret the pattern needs.
  • Use it to visualise where the next shape starts, not as a grab-and-strum barre.
Practice tipDon't expect a clean full barre. Keep the empty fret between the index block and the outer roots — don't squeeze the shape shut.

“Nobody bar-chords a full G-form in a gig. Learn its shape to connect the neck; play its corners.”
