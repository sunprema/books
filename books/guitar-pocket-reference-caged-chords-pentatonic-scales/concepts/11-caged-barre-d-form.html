The open D triangle moved up. A bright top-strings triad with its root on the 4th string.

Shape

  • Shown as E major: open D moved up 2 frets, x x 2 4 5 4.
  • Top four strings only; a compact movable triad.
  • Root on the 4th string (2nd fret = E).

When & why

  • High, chimey major voicings for arpeggios and fills.
  • Completes the CAGED loop above the G-form.
  • Great for adding a bright top to a full barre lower down.

Moves & transitions

  • Keep the strum on the top four strings — mute A and low E.
  • The G and D forms compress easily — keep the fret gaps.
  • Slide it to spell any major off the 4th-string root.
Practice tipMute the two bass strings with the underside of your fretting fingers so an accidental strum stays clean.

“Think of the D-form as a colour, not a foundation — a bright triad you sprinkle up high.”
