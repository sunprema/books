Chapter 01 · Foundations

What BookBank Is

Here’s the whole idea in one breath: you hand BookBank a topic and a voice, and you get back a small, beautiful, self-contained website that reads like a book — a cover, a table of contents, one page per idea that matters, and a cheatsheet at the end. It runs the research, picks the chapters, writes the prose, and designs the pages. You review it and, when you like it, publish it.

Think of it less as “a document editor” and more as “a co-author who reads the web, has good taste in layout, and never gets tired of drawing diagrams.”

Where it sits

BookBank is the generative member of a small family of tools (BankKit). What makes it generative is simple: the value isn’t a fixed template you fill in — it’s fresh content, researched and written on demand, wrapped in a design that fits the subject. Ask for a book on Rust and you get ownership diagrams; ask for one on calculus and you get a limit that animates. Same machinery, different book.

The one idea that matters A BookBank book is generated, not filled in. You describe what you want; the research, the chapter list, the words, and the layout are produced for you — then handed back as plain files you own.

The app does no thinking

This surprises people, so let’s be precise. The BookBank app (the macOS reader) does no AI work at all. It does two humble things: it writes down your request as a small file called book.json, and it displays the HTML that gets generated. All the actual authoring — the web research, the writing, the design — happens in Claude Code, driven by a plugin called bookbank.

That separation is the thing to hold onto. It means you don’t need the app to make a book. If you have Claude Code, you can request, write, and publish a book entirely from a terminal — the app is just a comfortable place to read and to drop in artwork. We’ll use both paths in this book, and call out which is which.

Everything stays local

A finished book is a folder of ordinary files — HTML, CSS, a little JavaScript, some images. There’s no server behind it and no database. The research happens over the web while the book is being written, but the book itself has no network dependencies: open it on a plane and every page, diagram, and animation still works. That’s a deliberate design choice, and it’s why a book is easy to share — you’re just sharing files.

You own the output Because a book is just files under a folder you control, you can read it in the app, drag index.html into any browser, keep it in git, or publish it to a public shelf. Nothing is locked inside a proprietary format.

What “good” means here

Two things, in order. First, accuracy: the content is grounded in real research — current facts, version-correct syntax, authoritative sources — not confidently-worded guesses. A book that’s beautiful but wrong is a failure. Second, a design that fits the subject: within one consistent house structure, each book gets a bespoke skin and diagrams that suit its topic. You’ll meet both dimensions in the chapters ahead — voice (the persona) and look (the theme).

So what did we just establish?

BookBank turns a topic and a voice into a researched, beautifully-designed book made of plain files you own. The app only records your request and displays the result — the authoring lives in the Claude Code plugin. Next we’ll open one of those folders and see exactly what a book is made of.
