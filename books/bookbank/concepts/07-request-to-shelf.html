Chapter 07 · The Pipeline

From Request to Shelf

Let’s follow one request the whole way. A book’s journey has four acts: it’s requested, then generated, then it gets its artwork, and finally it’s published to a public shelf. Understanding this arc tells you where any book is at any moment — and why nothing half-finished ever reaches the public site.

Act II: generation, in detail

This is the heart of it. Given a request, BookBank’s write-book skill does what a good author does:

  1. Reads the request — topic, persona, theme, any seed concepts and notes.
  2. Researches the web — current facts, version-correct syntax, primary sources. No invented APIs.
  3. Chooses the chapters — the 6–12 ideas that genuinely matter, ordered so each builds on the last (your seed concepts always included).
  4. Writes the pages — cover, one page per concept, and a cheatsheet, in the persona’s voice and the theme’s skin, with inline SVG diagrams.
  5. Declares image slots — placeholders for any real artwork it can’t draw (like a cover).
Two ways to run generation Locally, you run /write-book (or /create-book-from-issue <n>) yourself and open the PR when happy. In the cloud, an owner-only routine picks the oldest open request and does the whole round trip headlessly. Same skill, same output — only who presses go differs.

Act III: art before publish

A freshly-written book has image slots — a prompt and a reserved filename, but no picture yet. Rather than ship prompts to the public site, BookBank fills the art before publishing:

  • The book is pushed to a branch with a draft PR — never straight to the live site.
  • Each empty slot becomes a small image-request issue carrying its prompt.
  • Someone generates the art and attaches it; a maintainer approves it with a trusted art-approved label.
  • Automation places the image onto the book’s branch — no manual download-and-commit dance.
Why the ceremony? So the public shelf never shows a book full of “image needed” placeholders — or worse, caches your art prompts. The book goes live complete.

Act IV: onto the shelf

When the PR merges, publishing regenerates the library’s cover-card shelf and catalog from every book’s book.json, then the site deploys. Your book appears at a public URL alongside the rest:

the public library# the shelf
https://sunprema.github.io/books/

# your book
https://sunprema.github.io/books/books/<your-book-id>/

If you own the repo, one command does the publish step for a finished book:

publish a ready book (owner)/publish-library <your-book-id>
You don’t have to publish at all Publishing is optional. A book is fully usable the moment it’s ready — read it in the app, open it in a browser, or keep it entirely private. The shelf is just for sharing.

The whole arc

Request → generate (research + write) → art before publish → review & merge → live shelf. Every state is visible in book.json and on the issue/PR, and nothing incomplete slips out. Last stop: the practical recipes for making a book truly your own.

Source: BookBank write-book, create-book-from-issue, and publish-library skills; the art-before-publish image pipeline on sunprema/books.
