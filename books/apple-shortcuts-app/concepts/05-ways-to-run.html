Chapter 05 · Launch Surfaces

Every Way to Run a Shortcut

A shortcut is only as useful as it is reachable. Apple has quietly wired Shortcuts into more than a dozen launch surfaces across its devices — and the right one turns a good recipe into a reflex. Match the shortcut to the surface where the need actually strikes.

On iPhone & iPad

🗣 Siri Say the shortcut's name. Zero setup — the name is the phrase. Works hands-free, on CarPlay, and through AirPods.
◉ Action Button On iPhone 15 Pro and later, map the side button to any shortcut — the closest thing to a hardware macro key Apple sells.
▦ Widgets & Home Screen Add a Shortcuts widget (1, 4, or 8 buttons), or "Add to Home Screen" to give one recipe its own app-like icon.
⇪ Share Sheet Turn on "Show in Share Sheet" and your shortcut appears wherever you tap Share — receiving the photo, page, or file as input.
🔒 Lock Screen & Control Center Both are customizable with shortcut controls — replace the flashlight with your own button, reachable without unlocking the phone fully.
👆 Back Tap Settings → Accessibility → Touch → Back Tap: double- or triple-tap the back of the phone to fire a shortcut. The hidden fan favorite.
◎ NFC tag Technically an automation (chapter 6), but it feels like a launch surface: tap the phone on a sticker, the shortcut runs.
🔍 Spotlight Pull down, type the shortcut's name, hit go — fastest launcher when your hands are already on the keyboard.

On Apple Watch

Shortcuts marked Show on Apple Watch appear in the watch's Shortcuts app and can be added as complications on the watch face. Raise wrist, tap, done — ideal for logging (water, weight, medication) and toggles. Siri on the watch runs them by name too.

On Mac

Shortcuts has been a first-class Mac citizen since macOS Monterey, and the Mac gives it desktop-grade launchers:

  • Menu bar — pin shortcuts to a menu-bar menu, or add a Shortcuts control to the Mac's Control Center.
  • Spotlight — ⌘Space, type the name, return.
  • Keyboard shortcut — assign a global hotkey to any shortcut in its ⓘ panel. A text-cleanup recipe on ⌥⌘V is a daily-driver pattern.
  • Quick Actions — appear in Finder's right-click menu and the Services menu, receiving the selected files as input. This is the Mac's share sheet, and it's how batch file tools feel native.
  • The Dock — "Add to Dock" gives a shortcut an app-like tile.
Note

Your library syncs via iCloud, but launch surfaces are configured per device — the same "File This Receipt" shortcut can be a share-sheet action on iPhone, a Finder Quick Action on Mac, and a watch complication, all at once.

Choosing the right surface

If the moment is…Reach for
Hands busy / driving / cookingSiri, Action Button, Back Tap
"I'm looking at the thing" (photo, page, file)Share Sheet / Quick Action
Several times a day, same spotWidget, Lock Screen, Control Center, menu bar
A physical place (desk, kitchen, car)NFC tag, CarPlay/Bluetooth automation
On the wrist, two secondsWatch complication
Deep-work keyboard flow (Mac)Global hotkey, Spotlight
Key Idea

Friction decides whether a shortcut gets used. The recipe you run is the one that's one gesture away at the moment of need — placing the button well matters as much as building it well.
