Re-Parameterize & Serve

Training is done; the SwiGLU has served its purpose. Now we fold it into a table, build the lookup-only block, and stand up an inference server.

After training you hold a parameter map: m_l, the SwiGLU weights w_g/w_u/w_d, the gates w_gate/w_out, and the scalars. The export step from Chapter 5 walks each layer and bakes M̃l once:

elixir · export/reparameterizedef reparameterize_layer(p, e_global) do
  m_tilde =
    MeKi.NN.swiglu(e_global, p.w_g, p.w_u, p.w_d)
    |> Nx.multiply(p.beta)
    |> Nx.add(p.m_l)
    |> MeKi.NN.rms_norm()
    |> Nx.multiply(p.alpha)        # {|V|, d_mem}

  # ship only what inference needs
  %{m_tilde: m_tilde, w_gate: p.w_gate, w_out: p.w_out}
end

The inference block is now a shadow of the training one — no embedding, no SwiGLU, no α/β. Just a lookup and the gate:

elixir · inference forwarddefn meki_infer(h, tok, m_tilde, w_gate, w_out) do
  e = Nx.take(m_tilde, tok)             # ROM lookup
  g = Nx.sigmoid(Nx.dot(h, w_gate))
  MeKi.NN.rms_norm(Nx.dot(e + g, w_out))
end

What lives where

Per token, MeKi moves only d_mem × L values out of ROM — for a 28-layer model at d_mem = 256, that is 7,168 values ≈ 14 KB in float16. Against UFS 4.0's ~4.2 GB/s of mostly-idle read bandwidth, the table can be prefetched by token id while the NPU is busy with attention and the FFN. The active-parameter footprint in RAM is identical to the dense baseline — the extra capacity is sitting in storage.

On the BEAM

Elixir won't run on the phone's NPU, but it is an excellent host for the server-side or desktop deployment: wrap the model in Nx.Serving and you get automatic request batching, back-pressure, and distribution for free.

elixir · serve itserving =
  Nx.Serving.new(fn opts ->
    Nx.Defn.jit(&MeKi.Model.decode_step/2, opts)
  end)
  |> Nx.Serving.batch_size(16)
  |> Nx.Serving.client_preprocessing(&tokenize/1)

# in the supervision tree:
{Nx.Serving, serving: serving, name: MeKi.Serving}

# anywhere in the app — concurrent callers are batched:
Nx.Serving.batched_run(MeKi.Serving, prompt)
