Why It Works: The Evidence

A clever architecture is a hypothesis. Here are the paper's measurements — the headline, the benchmark gains, and the ablations that justify each design choice.

The headline result is a like-for-like substitution. All models were pre-trained from scratch on the same 50B-token sample of FineWeb-Edu, using Qwen3 architectures as the dense baselines. The claim:

🏁
The headline

MeKi-1.7B reaches an average zero-shot score of 59.7, statistically rivaling the 4B dense model's 60.5 — while decoding 2.26× faster on a Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Elite. A 1.7B-active model doing a 4B model's work, at small-model speed.

Per-benchmark, MeKi-1.7B's gains over its own 1.7B dense baseline are largest exactly where you'd hope — knowledge recall — and consistent on reasoning too:

Against other memory architectures — Per-Layer Embedding (PLE, from Gemma-3n) and Engram (n-gram phrase caching) — MeKi-1.7B's 59.7 average beats PLE by +2.7 and Engram by +1.8, with a clearly lower validation-loss curve. So the win is not merely “add storage”; the way MeKi integrates it matters.

The ablations, read honestly

Each design decision in Chapters 3–5 is backed by a measured comparison:

  • Both memories help. On 0.6B, static-only scores 54.8 (vs 53.0 baseline); combining static + dynamic adds a further +0.7 / +0.8 over either alone — they carry complementary information.
  • Parallel-to-FFN wins. Of four placements, parallel-to-FFN gives the best average and the lowest loss. Placing MeKi after a block turns its narrow dmem into an information bottleneck.
  • Additive-Sigmoid wins. Among additive/multiplicative × sigmoid/SiLU fusions, additive-sigmoid had the lowest training loss at both scales.
  • Memory size scales log-linearly. Sweeping dmem shows validation loss falling log-linearly with table size; the paper picks 128 (0.6B) and 256 (1.7B) as the cost/quality knee.
  • Training FLOPs you throw away still help. A linear Gl underperforms the SwiGLU — even though both fold to the same zero-cost table at inference. Spending more compute in training pays off.
🔬
A nice mechanism check

Using a LogitLens, the authors measure per-layer KL divergence to the final output distribution. MeKi's is lower at every layer — the model reaches confident predictions earlier in the stack, suggesting the injected vectors give real semantic anchoring, not just extra parameters.

⚖️
What the evidence is not

All numbers are the paper's own, on sub-4B models pre-trained on 50B tokens of educational text. There is no instruction-tuned or multi-lingual evaluation, and ROM weights grow real storage (the 1.7B variant adds ~1.09B stored values). Promising and well-ablated — but read as a research result, not a product spec.
