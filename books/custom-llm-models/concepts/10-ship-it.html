The pipeline in words

Download an open model → QLoRA-fine-tune it on your data → merge (or keep the adapter to hot-swap) → optionally quantize for a smaller/faster serve → run it on vLLM → call it from any OpenAI client. That's a custom LLM in production.

· 1

The decision you must make first: fine-tune, or RAG?

Before you fine-tune anything, ask what problem you actually have — because half the time the answer isn't fine-tuning at all.

Fine-tune to change behaviour

Style, tone, output format, instruction-following, a durable domain skill or vocabulary. It bakes patterns into the weights. Costly to update.

RAG to inject knowledge

Fresh, factual, frequently-changing, or proprietary facts. Retrieved at query time and placed in the prompt. Updatable without retraining; easy to cite.

📚
The exam analogy

Fine-tuning is a closed-book exam — the model internalizes how to behave. RAG is an open-book exam — it looks facts up. For injecting new facts, retrieval generally beats fine-tuning; for changing how the model acts, fine-tuning wins. They're complementary, not rivals.

🧪
Acme, decided

Acme's voice and tagging are behaviour → fine-tune. Acme's current prices and this week's outages are facts → RAG. The production system does both: a QLoRA'd Qwen for the house style, a retrieval layer for live facts. That combination is the mature pattern.

Choosing your base model

Two families cover almost every custom-LLM project in 2026:

FamilySizes & notes
Llama3.1 (8B/70B/405B), 3.2 (1B/3B + 11B/90B vision), 3.3 (70B). Ubiquitous ecosystem. Community License — a custom, source-available license with a >700M-MAU clause.
QwenQwen2.5 (0.5B–72B, mostly Apache-2.0; 3B & 72B are custom) and Qwen3 (0.6B–32B dense + MoE, all Apache-2.0). Fine size ladder; permissive license.
⚖️
A simple rule

Want the most permissive license and a small size to start? Qwen3 (Apache-2.0 across the board). Want maximum ecosystem, tooling, and community fine-tunes? Llama. Both fine-tune with the identical QLoRA recipe from Chapter 7 — only the model_id and the chat template differ.

⚠️
Read the license before you ship

"Open weights" is not the same as "open source." Check the specific model's license against your use — especially scale clauses (Llama's MAU threshold) and the two non-Apache Qwen2.5 sizes (3B, 72B). Free to download ≠ free for every purpose.
