The whole thing in a breath

A cell is a walled, self-assembling bag of water that stores instructions (DNA), spends energy (ATP) to build machines (proteins), maintains itself (recycling), and copies itself (division) — trillions of times over, with no one in charge. You are ~37 trillion of them, cooperating.

Seven ideas to walk away with

1) The membrane self-assembles — order for free from physics. 2) Every cell has the same DNA; bookmarks make the difference. 3) Energy is stored as an ion waterfall and harvested by a turbine. 4) Shipping addresses live inside the parts, not in a manager. 5) Machines are just shapes that change when fed energy. 6) Waste is resource you haven't disassembled. 7) A healthy body is trillions of cells agreeing not to be selfish — cancer is that agreement breaking.

Medicine that speaks cell

mRNA vaccines (hand the ribosome a blueprint) · CAR-T (reprogram immune cells) · CRISPR (edit the DNA letters) · chemo (freeze the microtubules dividing cells need) · stem-cell therapy (regrow tissue). Every one is a chapter of this book made into a tool.
