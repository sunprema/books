End-to-End Blueprint

Time to braid the three threads — the board, the Rust firmware, the Elixir brain — into one rope you can actually build this weekend.

We've examined each piece on its own. Now let's stand back and watch a single temperature reading make its whole journey: from a sensor pin on an S3, through the air, across a broker, into Elixir, onto a glowing dashboard — and a command finding its way back. If you understand this one trip, you understand the entire system, because everything else is just more of the same trip in parallel.

The life of one reading

  1. The S3 reads a sensor over I2C → 23.4 °C.
  2. Rust firmware wraps it: {"c":23.4}.
  3. publish to sensors/node-7/temp over Wi-Fi.
  4. The broker routes it to subscribers of sensors/+/temp.
  5. tortoise311 decodes it; PubSub broadcasts it.
  6. A LiveView chart updates; Ecto writes a row.
  7. A rule fires a command back down sensors/node-7/cmd.
🔑
The whole system, honestly

Rust firmware on the edge → MQTT over Wi-Fi → a broker → Elixir/Phoenix on the server. Lean and safe at the edge; calm and massively concurrent at the center. Each side does what it's best at.

🧪
Test the seam before the chip

You can build steps 4–8 entirely without an ESP32 — just publish fake readings from your laptop with mosquitto_pub. Get the backend green first, then swap in the real device. Debugging one unknown at a time is the whole game.

Topics, security & scale

A topic convention

Pick a hierarchy early and stick to it — wildcards depend on it:

topic schemesensors/<node-id>/temp     # readings up
sensors/<node-id>/status   # online/offline (LWT)
sensors/<node-id>/cmd      # commands down

Don't ship it naked

  • TLS — use mqtts:// (port 8883); the S3 has the crypto to do it.
  • Auth — per-device username/password or client certs at the broker.
  • Least privilege — ACLs so a node may publish only its own topics.
