KeepsakeThe Moral Chest

Every tale leaves behind a little treasure of wisdom. Here they all are, kept in one chest — perfect for reading aloud, or remembering the next time you need a clever idea of your own.

The Gift of Goddess Kali

A brave boy laughs instead of trembling before the Goddess, and chooses wisdom over gold.

★ Be brave, keep your good humour, and choose the gift no one can take from you — a clever, kind mind.

The Thirsty Thieves

Tenali tricks two robbers into hauling well-water all night, watering his whole garden for free.

★ A good idea beats brute strength — and greed will make a person work hard for a treasure that was never there.

The Cat That Feared Milk

Free milk made every cat lazy; only Tenali's hungry cat actually caught the rats.

★ Think about what a rule will really make people do. Rewarding folk for doing nothing rarely gets the job done.

The Secret of the Brinjal Curry

A blindfolded boy happily eats the very vegetable he had vowed never to touch.

★ Don't boast about promises you've never had to keep. Real strength is understanding why you do right.

The Three Little Dolls

With one thread, Tenali shows which doll listens and keeps counsel — and which lets everything slip away.

★ The best person listens well and keeps a secret safe. Good listening is worth more than gold.

One Last Thing to Remember

In every single tale, Tenali Raman wins the same way — not with a sword, not with strength, but by stopping to think when everyone else is busy panicking. That is a kind of magic you can practise too.

A Note for Grown-ups

Tenali Raman (Tenali Ramakrishna) was a real 16th-century Telugu poet in Emperor Krishnadevaraya's Vijayanagara court, one of the eight "Ashtadiggajas." The tales here are beloved folk stories, retold in many versions over five hundred years — read them aloud, and add your own spice.
