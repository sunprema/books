The twelve notes

C C♯ D D♯ E F F♯ G G♯ A A♯ B

No note between B–C and E–F. Half step = 1 fret. Whole step = 2 frets. Octave = 12 frets = 2× the frequency.

1 m22 M23 m34 M35 P46 TT
7 P58 m69 M610 m711 M712 P8

Happy = M3 (4). Sad = m3 (3). Strong = P5 (7).

Chords & scales

  • Major triad = 1 + M3 + P5 (e.g. C–E–G).
  • Minor triad = 1 + m3 + P5 (e.g. A–C–E).
  • Major scale = W–W–H–W–W–W–H (C major = C D E F G A B).
  • Minor pentatonic = 1–♭3–4–5–♭7 (A min pent = A C D E G; box at fret 5).

Mateo's practice laws

Practice like you debug code
  • Slow is smooth. Smooth becomes fast. Tempo is earned, not forced.
  • Tune first. A great chord on an out-of-tune guitar still sounds wrong.
  • Practice the change, not just the chord.
  • Metronome early. Rhythm before melody.
  • Short and daily beats long and rare. Isolate the one broken thing and fix it.
  • Don't stare at your fingers — listen.

Glossary

  • Fret — a metal bar; pressing behind it shortens the string, raising pitch a half step.
  • Root — the note a chord or scale is named after and built on.
  • Triad — a 3-note chord: root, third, fifth.
  • Interval — the distance between two notes, in half steps.
  • Key — the family of notes/chords a song lives in.
  • Capo — a clamp that raises every string, shifting your open shapes to a new key.
