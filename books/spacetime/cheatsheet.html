The one idea, five ways

  • Gravity is geometry. Not a force — the curvature of spacetime.
  • Equivalence principle. Free-fall = weightless; a sealed box can’t tell gravity from acceleration.
  • Wheeler’s sentence. Matter tells spacetime how to curve; spacetime tells matter how to move.
  • Geodesics. Falling objects go perfectly straight — through curved spacetime. Orbits are straight lines on a bent stage.
  • Time is personal. Clocks run slower when moving fast and when deep in gravity.

Two observers, one black hole

Both stories are true You (falling in): cross the horizon at a definite moment feeling nothing; then every path leads to the center; tides stretch you (“spaghettification”).
Me (watching): I see you slow, redden, and freeze at the horizon, fading to invisibility — never quite crossing, in my time.

The evidence

  • 1919 — starlight bends around the eclipsed Sun (Eddington).
  • 1915 — Mercury’s 43″/century orbit shift explained exactly.
  • Every day — GPS works only by correcting +38 µs/day.
  • 2015 — LIGO hears two black holes merge (GW150914); ~3 M☉ radiated as waves.
  • 2019 / 2022 — Event Horizon Telescope images M87* then Sgr A*.
