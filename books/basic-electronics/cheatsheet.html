A radio, in three steps

  1. Tune — an LC tank (antenna coil + variable capacitor) resonates at one station's frequency.
  2. Detect — a diode rectifies the carrier; an RC filter smooths it into the audio envelope.
  3. Amplify — a transistor (or, historically, a triode) boosts the recovered audio to speaker volume.

30-second summary

Resistors set current for a given voltage. Capacitors store charge and block DC while passing AC. Inductors store current in a magnetic field and block sudden changes. Diodes pass current one way only. Triodes and transistors both use a small control signal to throttle a much larger current — that's amplification. A radio is all five ideas, wired in sequence: tune, detect, amplify.
